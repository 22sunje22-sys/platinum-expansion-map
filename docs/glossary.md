# Glossary

Deck-specific jargon used across the map and docs.

## Market concepts

**Klook belt** — The Asian incumbent moat. Klook dominates Japan · Singapore · Malaysia · Thailand · Hong Kong for attractions + experiences. "Breaking the Klook belt" = winning share in any Asian market. Singapore entry is explicitly *family-tech niche vs Klook's adult segments* — wedge, not frontal attack.

**Burj-class** — Iconic, pre-bookable, brandable supply — named after Burj Khalifa as the reference SKU. Burj-class attractions have: (a) global brand recognition, (b) native online pre-booking, (c) authorized reseller API. The supply lens is really a "how many Burj-class anchors can we contract?" audit.

**Regulatable-open** — State-controlled supply that still has a digital B2B surface (API, reseller agreement). Jordan Pass B2B API is the paradigm example. Distinguishes *regulated* from *closed*.

**Regulatable-closed** — State-controlled supply with no B2B surface. Greek hhticket.gr is the paradigm example — every top Greek attraction routes through it, no reseller API, no cache-ticket primitive. Adjacent to *state-gated*.

**State-gated** — Same as regulatable-closed. Used when emphasizing the DQ implication (1 Turkey-flag, Watchlist tier).

**MoTA monopoly** — Ministry of Tourism & Antiquities single-platform control. Egypt is the active example (3 Turkey-flags). Turkey has a MoTA-equivalent.

**Khaleeji Vienna** — Recurring GCC summer travel pattern (Jun–Aug) centered on Vienna (cool climate, halal-friendly, family-oriented). Generalizes to Khaleeji Zurich · Khaleeji Interlaken · Khaleeji Salzburg. Drives the Austria / Switzerland moat thesis.

## Scoring terms

**Moat (measurable)** — GCC bias ratio = GCC search intent ÷ worldwide search intent. High ratio = market with GCC-skewed demand that generic OTAs (Klook, GYG, Tiqets) don't service natively. Malaysia 0.21× · Singapore 0.14× · Jordan 0.11× are the shortlist leaders.

**Full-GCC TAM** (Pass 2D) — Demand measured across all 6 GCC countries (UAE+KSA+KW+QA+OM+BH) rather than UAE+KSA proxy. Small Gulf states add 23–53% more demand and skew toward European boutique markets.

**Composite (v2.1)** — Base score /100 (Demand 30 + Supply 20 + Moat 20 + Econ 20 + Strategic 10) plus Catalyst overlay /10 → /110.

**Turkey-flag** — One of 5 auto-DQ criteria (state monopoly · FX vol >25% · PSP blocked · pre-book <5/10 · incumbent SOV >60%). ≥2 flags = auto-DQ.

**Catalyst overlay** — +10/+8/+5/+3/0 layer reflecting near-term directional events (mega-event + air corridor + government campaign). See [scorecard.md](scorecard.md).

## Operational terms

**Tour-led vs ticket-led** — Tour-led markets sell experiences bundled with a guide (Morocco, Jordan, Egypt). Ticket-led markets sell standalone attraction access (Italy, Spain, Singapore). Platinumlist model indexes on ticket-led; tour-led markets need partnership-mode.

**Single-anchor market** — Market where demand concentrates on one SKU (Poland: Auschwitz 400 GCC queries/mo). High concentration = fragile portfolio.

**Execution play** — Market with thin moat but high iconic supply — compete on UX/Arabic/positioning, not discovery. Italy and Spain are the examples.

**Paid-test market** — Low-CPC market used to validate paid-acquisition hypotheses before committing to higher-CPC markets. Spain ($0.25–1.50 range) is positioned as paid-test after Italy learnings.

**Direct-lock inventory** — Supply that requires carrier-level agreements to access (not OTA). Shibuya Sky and teamLab in Japan are the archetypes.

**Contractable share** — % of top-SKUs reachable through reseller contracts within a 6-month window. Malaysia ≥80%, Italy 60–70% (state concessions slow contracting), Greece <20% (state-gated).

**Ramp factor** — Conservative discount applied to traffic × conversion for the launch ramp period. 0.4× (affiliate / execution play) · 0.5× (direct anchor). See [financial-model.md](financial-model.md).
